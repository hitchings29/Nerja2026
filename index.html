Arrival Tip

The drive from Malaga airport to Nerja takes about 45-50 minutes straight down the A-7 highway. Make sure to buy large 5L or 8L bottles of water (Agua Mineral) during your grocery run—tap water is safe, but bottled tastes much better for tea and baby formula!

About Frigiliana

Consistently voted one of the most beautiful villages in Spain. Its Moorish old town is a maze of perfectly preserved, flower-draped streets. Look out for the local sweet wine ('Vino de Frigiliana') and cane honey ('Miel de Caña'), uniquely produced here.

Mezquita History & Food

The Great Mosque was started in 784 AD. After the Christian conquest of Córdoba in 1236, it was converted into a Catholic church, culminating in the insertion of a Renaissance cathedral nave right in its center in the 16th century! After visiting, grab a famous extra-thick 'Tortilla de Patatas' at Bar Santos right outside the walls.

Market Finds & Churros

At the Tuesday market (located in the Almijara area), look out for vibrant Andalusian ceramics, fresh local olives, and spices. It's a busy, colorful affair. Afterwards, treat yourselves to traditional Spanish Churros con Chocolate at a local café—a perfectly sweet midday energy boost.

Setenil de las Bodegas

A stunning town built into the cliffs. Stick to the riverbed streets for easier stroller access.

Ronda

Head to the Alameda del Tajo park for a flat, shaded walk that leads to incredible balconies overlooking the valley and bridge.

Note: A carrier for Aelin is best for gorge paths.

Engineering Marvels

Ronda's 'Puente Nuevo' (New Bridge) is over 100 meters deep and took 34 years to build, finally completing in 1793. Meanwhile, in Setenil, the unique houses aren't just built *next* to rocks—they utilize natural overhangs as their actual roofs, a design that keeps them naturally cool during the scorching Andalusian summers.

Thu 30 Apr • Grand Highlight

Granada: The Alhambra

The crown jewel of Andalusia. The Nasrid Palaces require a specific time slot—be punctual!

Alhambra Rules

Palaces: Strollers are forbidden inside the palaces; you'll need the carrier.

Gardens: The Generalife gardens are mostly accessible with wheels.

Essential: Do your big grocery shop today! Tomorrow is a holiday.

The Red Fortress

The Alhambra (from Arabic 'Al-Ḥamrāʾ', meaning 'The Red One') was the last Moorish stronghold in Spain, falling to the Catholic Monarchs in 1492. It inspired Washington Irving's famous 'Tales of the Alhambra'. Pay close attention to the intricate, mathematical perfection of the stucco work and the soothing use of water features throughout.

Día del Trabajador

May 1st is taken very seriously in Spain. Supermarkets and major attractions will be firmly shut. However, restaurants and beach 'Chiringuitos' often stay open and get busy with local families. If you fancy venturing out, head to Burriana beach for a massive, wood-fire cooked Paella at the famous 'Ayo'.

Discovering Nerja

Unlike much of the Costa del Sol, Nerja has escaped the high-rise concrete hotels. It retains a traditional charm with its whitewashed houses and narrow streets.

  • Balcón de Europa: A magnificent promenade built on the edge of a cliff, offering panoramic views of the Mediterranean. Great for evening strolls.
  • Tapas Culture: In many traditional Nerja bars, you still get a free 'tapa' (small snack) with every drink you order.

Spanish Culture & Dining

  • The Siesta: It's real! Many small shops and businesses close between 14:00 and 17:00. Plan shopping accordingly.
  • Dining Times: Spaniards eat late. Lunch is the main meal (around 14:00). Dinner rarely starts before 20:30 or 21:00.
  • Tipping: A 10% tip is appreciated for good service in sit-down restaurants, but not strictly mandatory. For cafes/bars, just leave the small change.

First Time Baby Tips

  • Bring a baby carrier! Strollers are great for promenades, but useless in the Alhambra palaces or steep Frigiliana streets.
  • Spanish supermarkets (Mercadona, Supersol) have excellent baby aisles for nappies and formula.
  • Dining out is very baby-friendly in Spain; locals love kids and restaurants usually accommodate high chairs.

Local Travel Info

  • Always carry some cash (Euros) for small parking lots or rural cafes.
  • Toll roads (AP-7) accept card payments and are usually much faster and quieter than coastal roads.

Health & Emergencies

  • Emergency: Dial 112 for all emergencies (operators speak English).
  • Pharmacies: Look for the glowing green cross ("Farmacia"). They are highly trained and can advise on minor ailments for the baby. There is always an "on-duty" 24hr pharmacy listed on the door of closed ones.
